Art

This page is about design, photography, and junk metal sculptures.

Design at Stanford

I concentrated in Human Computer Interaction (HCI) and was the HCI course advisor my senior year at Stanford. I dabble in web design and product design, but the softest spot in my heart belongs to human centered design. My design coursework at Stanford included:

  • Design for Extreme Affordability
  • Design for Exploration
  • Introduction to Human Values in Design
  • Introduction to Human Computer Interaction
  • Fundamential Visual Language

Sculptures

I started my first junk metal sculpture when I was six years old. I've had the great fortune of inheriting access to a farm replete with very decrepid, very rusty metal. Even after my exposure to the labs and materials available at Stanford, I still mostly like working with rusty metal.

Dolphin

Old bicycle frame was used to make the body, beak, and melon.

Mirror Cube

Two way mirrors, dichroic glass, lights, fishing wire.

Crane

Sink pipe, auger bits, compression pipe (from a fish cannery destroyed by the Good Friday Alaska Earthquake of 1964)

Cowgirl

Pedal sewing machine frame, garbage disposal, baler pickups, hub covers, stirrups, bedpan, anchor, chain, box end wrenches, mattress springs.

Metal Motifs

Rusty gears, saw blades, nuts and bolts, hub covers, punch, awn, sickle bar and mower teeth.

SPARK! Photography Grant

I received a grant to photograph young artists training to be circus performers in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The photos were exhibited at Stanford during winter and spring quarter of the 2013-14 academic year.

Teaching

I taught social circus, including aerial silks and aerial hoop to low income youth in Rio de Janeiro. I taught therapeutic dance to inmates in San Mateo Juvenile Hall through the Dance in Prison program led by dance critic Janice Ross.

My Thoughts on Prison Reform

Sculpture Lab Assistant

I was the Sculpture Lab Assistant for two years at Stanford, where I kept sleep deprived undergrads from sawing their fingers off in the wood shop or plasma torching their limbs in the metal shop. Pictured are some glasses that I made to see the world upside down.

Kinetic Sculpture

I helped build kinetic sound sculptures, including a harp made with guitar strings and played with falling water, for German artist Trimpin's work 'Gurs Zyklus,' which brought to life the work of a man confined in a concentration camp near Trimpin's home town.

Circus

Circus is my main artistic gig. I'm an aerialist (more specifically, I'm pretty good at aerial silks, aerial hoop, and static trapeze), but I'm also a mediocre juggler and unicyclist.
